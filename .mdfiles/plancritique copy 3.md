One gap remains, and it's in the same family as the earlier two: a piece of state the backward needs isn't listed among what gets saved. Step 1 of UlyssesAttentionBackward::apply branches on "if the forward unsharded" to decide whether to narrow the incoming grad_out down to this rank's local slice, or take it as-is. But the list of saved state — q_g, k_g, v_g, out_g, lse, the all-to-all metadata, pg_, scale, is_causal, and the original input shapes — doesn't include the unshard boolean itself, which is the only thing that condition can branch on. Without it persisted on the node, that branch has nothing to check at backward time. The two implicit alternatives both have problems: inferring it from grad_out's shape works but is fragile (it makes correctness depend on the caller never accidentally passing a wrongly-shaped gradient, and it's the kind of implicit contract that tends to bite later when someone adds a third shape variant), and assuming ContextParallelBackward already solves this elsewhere and reuse will pull it in for free is exactly the kind of assumption that points 1 and 2 already showed can silently misfire for Ulysses specifically.
Given the verification plan now explicitly drives both unshard=true and unshard=false through this exact backward path, this isn't hypothetical — the ws=2/ws=4 parity test will exercise both branches of that condition, so whatever the node uses to decide needs to actually be there. I'd add one line to the "Saves" list: persist unshard_ (the flag passed into forward_ulysses) on UlyssesAttentionBackward alongside the rest of the saved state, and have step 1 branch on that explicitly rather than on inferred shape.
That's the only outstanding item — everything else holds up.
